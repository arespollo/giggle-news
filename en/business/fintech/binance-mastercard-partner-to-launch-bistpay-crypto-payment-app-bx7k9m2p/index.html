Binance and Mastercard partner to launch BistPay, a crypto-to-fiat payment app for 90+ markets

Joint venture marks the deepest integration between a global payments network and a crypto exchange, enabling instant spending at millions of merchants worldwide

Gulf News Report Published: March 25, 2026 10:58 AM GST
f 𝕏 ✆ in ✉
Binance and Mastercard announced the co-development of BistPay, combining crypto infrastructure with global payment rails.
Image Credit: Supplied

Binance, the world's largest cryptocurrency exchange by trading volume, and Mastercard have announced a strategic partnership to co-develop BistPay, a mobile payment application designed to enable seamless crypto-to-fiat transactions at the point of sale across more than 90 countries.

The collaboration marks the deepest integration to date between a major global payments network and a crypto-native platform. Under the terms of the agreement, BistPay will leverage Mastercard's established payment rails and merchant network, while Binance provides the underlying digital asset infrastructure — including custody, real-time conversion, and multi-jurisdictional compliance.

"Users shouldn't have to think about whether they're spending crypto or fiat. BistPay removes that friction entirely."

— Binance spokesperson

How BistPay works

The app will allow users to hold Bitcoin, Ethereum, BNB, stablecoins, and select altcoins within a unified wallet, and spend them instantly at any Mastercard-accepting merchant worldwide. Real-time conversion will occur at the point of transaction, with users viewing both crypto and fiat balances in a single interface.

Key features announced include:

  • Instant crypto-to-fiat conversion at point of sale with no manual swaps required
  • Virtual and physical Mastercard-branded cards linked directly to users' crypto wallets
  • Multi-currency support including BTC, ETH, BNB, USDT, USDC, and regional fiat currencies
  • Zero-fee transactions for stablecoin payments during the initial launch period
  • Built-in compliance with KYC and AML standards across all operating jurisdictions

Strategic significance

Mastercard's Chief Digital Officer noted that the partnership reflects the company's broader strategy to integrate digital assets into its existing financial infrastructure rather than building a parallel system.

"We're not building a parallel system. We're embedding crypto into the financial system that already works for billions of people."

— Mastercard Chief Digital Officer

Unlike previous Binance card programmes that relied on third-party issuers, BistPay represents a direct co-development between the two entities, with shared technology stacks and compliance responsibilities. Industry analysts view the arrangement as a significant signal for institutional acceptance of cryptocurrency payments.

Launch timeline

The Binance-Mastercard collaboration targets seamless crypto spending across 90+ markets.
Image Credit: Binance

BistPay is expected to launch in beta across the UAE, Singapore, and select European markets in Q3 2026, with a full global rollout planned for early 2027. Both companies have committed to meeting local regulatory requirements in each jurisdiction prior to launch.

The move comes amid a broader trend of traditional financial institutions deepening ties with crypto platforms. Visa, Mastercard's primary competitor, has separately expanded its stablecoin settlement capabilities, while PayPal continues to grow its PYUSD stablecoin ecosystem.

Neither Binance nor Mastercard disclosed the financial terms of the partnership. Sources familiar with the arrangement suggest both parties have committed significant engineering resources, with a dedicated team of more than 200 developers working across Dubai, New York, and Singapore.

BistPay will be available for download on iOS and Android upon launch. A waitlist is expected to open in the coming weeks through the official Binance and BistPay websites.
